What Ge’ez Stands For

The name Ge’ez signifies "the beginning"—a fitting symbol for our vision to lead a new era in biotechnology. At Ge’ez Bio, we are committed to pioneering the local production of high-quality enzymes, bio-based fertilizers, and eco-friendly pesticides that are safe for both human health and the planet. As import costs soar and demand continues to rise, we believe Ethiopia is ready to embrace a homegrown biotech movement—one driven by science, sustainability, and self-reliance. Ge’ez Bio is here to help shape that future.

Our Story

Our journey began with a shared concern: the undeniable harm that chemical-based fertilizers and pesticides are causing to both human health and the environment. We couldn’t look away from the long-term damage—soil degradation, water contamination, and the growing health risks tied to the food we consume. With Lydia’s background in medicine and Melaku’s in biochemistry, we found a natural synergy. Together, we envisioned a more holistic approach—one that protects human well-being, nourishes the earth, and uplifts local communities. Ge’ez Bio was born from that vision: a commitment to creating safe, and effective alternatives. We believe that healthy food starts with healthy soil, and that true progress must be rooted in science, sustainability, and a deep respect for life. Our work is not just about replacing chemicals—it’s about restoring balance and dignity to the way we grow, eat, and live.

We are scientists, farmers, engineers, and dreamers. Every product we release is not just a formula—it’s a small piece of a larger mission: to give Ethiopia control over what it grows, builds, and exports. Our journey is just beginning, and we invite you to be part of it.

Vision & Mission

  • Vision: To ignite a new beginning by empowering Ethiopia to lead with local innovation, holistic health, and sustainable agriculture.
  • Mission: Inspired by the name “Ge’ez”—meaning origin—we aim to transform the way Ethiopia produces and uses enzymes, biofertilizers, and biopesticides.
